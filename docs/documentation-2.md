Final Procurement Process Sequence – PH Government

STEP 1: Identify Need

Teacher or end-user identifies the need (e.g., tables, whiteboard).

Request submitted to Supply Office.

STEP 2: Check Inventory

Decision: Is item available in stock?

Yes:

Issue RIS (Requisition and Issue Slip).

Deliver item immediately.

Process ends. Status: RIS Completed – Item Issued.

No:

Forward request to Procurement / BAC Secretariat.

Proceed to create or update PPMP.

Status: Item Not Available – Procurement Needed.

STEP 3: Create or Update PPMP

End-user unit prepares PPMP entry:

Item description, quantity, estimated cost, target month.

Applies to planned items and unplanned/new items.

Status: PPMP Created/Updated – New or Supplemental Item.

STEP 4: Submit PPMP to Budget Office

Budget Office reviews PPMP.

Decision: Is there available budget or can funds be allocated?

Yes:

Proceed to BAC Secretariat for APP consolidation.

No:

Item may be deferred for next budget cycle.

Status: Pending PPMP – Waiting for Budget.

Legal Note: Fund allocation for unplanned items is lawful as long as funds are available or reallocated properly, in compliance with GAA and COA rules.

STEP 5: BAC Secretariat – Consolidate PPMP into APP

BAC Secretariat consolidates PPMPs into draft APP.

HoPE approval of APP.

Status: APP Approved – Items Eligible for Procurement.

STEP 6: Prepare Purchase Request (PR)

Requesting office prepares PR referencing approved APP.

PR submitted for approval.

Status: PR Submitted – Pending Approval.

STEP 7: PR Review and Approval

Reviewed by:

Unit / School Head

Budget Officer (fund verification)

Accounting Office (if required)

Decision: Is PR approved?

Yes: proceed to procurement.
No: return to requester for correction.

Status: PR Approved.

STEP 8: Procurement Process

Select procurement method (e.g., Shopping / RFQ / Canvas for small-value items).

Issue RFQ / Canvas / Invitation to Bid.

Suppliers submit quotations / bids.

Evaluate quotations / bids → prepare Abstract of Quotations / Bid Evaluation Report.

BAC prepares Recommendation for Award → submitted to HoPE.

HoPE approves award.

Issue Purchase Order (PO) → supplier accepts.

System status updates:

Procurement Method Selected

RFQ/Canvas Issued

Quotations Received

Abstract of Quotations Prepared

Award Approved

PO Issued

PO Accepted – Procurement Started

STEP 9: Delivery of Items

Supplier delivers items to end-user.

Status: Delivery Completed – Pending Inspection.

STEP 10: Inspection and Acceptance

Inspection and Acceptance Committee (IAC) checks:

Quantity, quality, condition.

Prepares Inspection and Acceptance Report (IAR).

Status: Items Accepted – IAR Prepared.

STEP 11: Supplier Billing and Payment

Supplier submits invoices and supporting documents to Accounting.

Accounting verifies and prepares Disbursement Voucher (DV).

DV submitted for signatures.

Decision: Is DV signed?

Yes:

Issue cheque or payment.

Tag procurement as complete.

No:

Return for correction/signature.

Status: Payment Completed – Procurement Closed.

✅ Summary Notes

RIS is only issued if items are available in stock.

All procurements must be included in PPMP and APP, even for unplanned or supplemental needs.

Budget Office can lawfully allocate funds for items not in original budget.

BAC oversees procurement compliance with RA 9184.

PR, PO, IAR, and payment steps are tracked for system status updates.
